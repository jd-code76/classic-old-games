Brief History of Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth

Originally released on December 6th, 2004 by EA Games, The Battle for Middle-earth is a real-time strategy game developed by EA Los Angeles. It was built using the SAGE engine, which was also used for Command & Conquer: Generals. The game features four factions: Gondor, Rohan, Isengard, and Mordor, each with unique units, heroes, and abilities. The campaign follows the events of the film trilogy, allowing players to experience major battles from both good and evil perspectives.

However, it was also notorious for its crazy development schedule and awful working conditions. The crunch was so bad that many developers had to spend upwards of 60-80+ hours per week working on the game without sleep. This prompted a multi-million dollar lawsuit that eventually ended in EA settling for nearly $15 million.

In spite of the horrible working conditions, the game was a critical and commercial success, being praised for its faithful adaptation of the source material, impressive visuals and solid RTS gameplay. It sold over 2 million copies, leading to the development of a sequel and expansion pack.
