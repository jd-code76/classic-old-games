Brief History of Metroid: Samus Returns

Originally released on September 15, 2017, for the Nintendo 3DS, Metroid: Samus Returns is a remake of Metroid II: Return of Samus, developed by MercurySteam. It was acclaimed for modernizing the 1991 Game Boy title with 3D visuals, new mechanics like the melee counter, and expanded content, breathing new life into a lesser-known entry of the series.

Gameplay follows Samus Aran on SR388 the Metroid homeworld. New abilities like the melee counter, aeion powers, and 360 degree free-aim shooting enhance combat, while traditional power-ups like the morph ball and spider ball enable broad exploration. The game emphasizes backtracking and discovery, with updated boss fights and a refined control scheme, making it a standout but repetitive remake. MercurySteam would learn from this release and use that experience to create Metroid Dread for the Switch just a few years later.
