Brief History of Metroid II: Return of Samus

Originally released in November 1991 for the Game Boy, Metroid II: Return of Samus is the second game in the Metroid series. It is considered a classic for bringing Metroid to portable gaming, introducing key series elements like the spider ball, the ability to shoot down while jumping, and crouching. It was a very ambitious game despite the Game Boy’s limitations, laying much of the groundwork for future entries in the series.

Like the original game, gameplay follows Samus Aran on the Metroid home world of SR388. Tasked with exterminating all of the Metroids, players acquire abilities like the morph ball, varia suit, and spazer beam, unlocking new areas through a linear exploration. Also like the first game, the plater can only utilize one beam at a time, something that would be changed with future entries in the series. Combat involves platforming and shooting, with Metroid encounters serving as pseudo-boss fights. The monochromatic visuals and limited hardware create a stark atmosphere, but the game’s tight design and exploration focus make it a foundational title in the series.
