Brief History of Metroid: Zero Mission

Originally released on February 9, 2004, for the Game Boy Advance, Metroid: Zero Mission is a remake of the original Metroid using the Fusion engine. It was critically acclaimed for modernizing the 1986 classic with updated graphics, refined controls and new content like the Zero Suit segment, appealing to both new players and veteran fans. Zero Mission strikes an excellent balance of nostalgia and innovation, preserving the original’s exploration while enhancing accessibility for a modern audience.

Like Fusion, the gameplay of Zero Mission focuses on Samus Aran exploring Zebes in a non-linear Metroidvania structure just like the original game. Players gain abilities like missiles, morph ball, and high jump, unlocking new paths through backtracking. Combat emphasizes precise platforming and shooting, with new boss fights and expanded areas adding variety. The game’s polished mechanics, vibrant visuals and additional post-game content make it a standout remake in the series and an excellent example of what any good remake should be.
