Brief History of Quake III: Arena

Originally released on December 2nd, 1999 for Windows, Quake III was a game with a lot of "firsts." It was the first game in the series to not have a single-player campaign, featuring online and local multiplayer gameplay (although AI bots are supported). It was the first game in the series to require a discrete graphics card which was largely unheard of at the time. It was the first game to utilize the id Tech 3 engine (the same engine that lives on in Call of Duty and other FPS games).

Quake III was revolutionary for the FPS genre much in the same way that the original Quake was. The multiplayer in Quake III was known for being the most refined version of Quake at the time and to this day, people still play it. Quake III also significantly increased the presence of competitive gamers and eSports teams, with large tournaments being held annually. An expansion pack titled "Team Arena" was released by id on December 18th, 2000. Team Arena focuses on team oriented multiplayer modes and contains a single-player mode as well as bots.

Quake II Source Ports

ioquake3 - A source port for Quake III that runs on Linux, Windows, and Mac. Great for those who want to experience Quake III as vanilla as possible.
Quake3e - A source port for Quake III that runs on Linux, Windows, Raspberry Pi, and Mac. Adds quality-of-life features to make the game more modern.
Spearmint - A source port for Quake III that is based on ioquake3. It is not being developed anymore (since 2018), but does still have some features that people may find useful.
