Brief History of Halo 2

Originally released on November 9th, 2004 for the original Xbox, Halo 2 vastly improved on the success of Halo: CE. The game is the first in the franchise to have dual-wielding and vehicle boarding. It is also the first Halo game to have Xbox Live multiplayer, with Bungie developing several features like voice chat, groups, and clans. These features later became staples of the Xbox Live service.

Halo 2 is known for having a large advertising campaign. Bungie spent so much time and resources on advertising, that they ended up cutting a large part of the singleplayer campaign. Ultimately, the campaign ended up being good, but many players complained about the cliffhanger ending. Halo 2 was the most played game on Xbox Live until Halo 3 was released three years later.
